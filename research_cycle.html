Project Name

Physics of Sports

I participated in the Physics of Sports class at Harvard while in my Senior Sping (in its first time running) and joined the teaching staff the following year. This engineering class explores athlete biomechanics through experiment-based learning. As a teaching fellow, I guide students in devising their own experiments, ranging from refining sprinter block settings based on individual body proportions to enhancing rowing efficiency by analyzing seat acceleration profiles. While students had access to advanced tools like the motion capture lab, their experiments predominantly utilized IMU sensors for data collection. I played an active role in developing the course's curriculum by crafting the code to compile, organize, and analyze this sensor-acquired data.
